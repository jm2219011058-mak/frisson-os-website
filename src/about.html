About Us · More than Human City

Collecting the Dreams of the Whole Earth

Great cities have never been made great by accumulating the most buildings or the most stone. A city becomes truly great when, after wandering for five centuries, beauty chooses to return there — and begins again, among the most creative souls of humanity.

We believe that beauty itself possesses the power to migrate.

It travels across geographies, wars, and the turning of civilizations, pausing only in the rarest of moments. We pay homage to the places where it once chose to dwell.

Florence was once its waystation — as were the fields of Devonshire, the seventh hill of Lisbon, and the forests of Île-de-France.

We graft into our work the aesthetic legacies of four great European Renaissance houses — Medici, Cavendish, Palmela, and Montmorency — translating their spirit into a contemporary language of creation.

Florence is Brunelleschi’s proportions, the blue-grey stone of pietra serena, Raphael’s frescoes, and Buontalenti’s artificial grotto in the Boboli Gardens. Chatsworth is a book that has never been fully written — from William Kent’s designs and Paxton’s glasshouse to Lucian Freud’s portraits.

The Palmela Palace holds Anatole Calmels’ herms, the illusions of trompe-l'œil, the Eastern motifs of chinoiserie, and the delicate warmth of blue-and-white azulejos.

Montmorency’s Écouen is a fortress that breathes, gathering the finest hands of an entire age — Jean Bullant’s loggia, Jean Goujon’s sculptures, Léonard Limosin’s Limoges enamel, and the ceramics of Bernard Palissy and Masséot Abaquesne.

Our ultimate purpose is to restore something far older — and far more precious:

Respect for Humanity.

Beyond the Canvas

Let art leave the canvas.

Let it enter architecture, enter light, enter the body, and ultimately enter the human soul.

We bring the imagination of contemporary artists into architectural details and share it with the world.

Through works such as Jesperish’s creations — which explore identity, emotion, and symbolic systems through a perception of an ancient future — we awaken intuition and explore how architecture, culture, and environment shape the inner world of humanity.

What humans truly desire has never been beauty placed on display.

Not a Fantasy of ‘More-than-Human’

The true meaning of “more” is not to surpass humanity, but to understand humanity more deeply — and to honour it.

Because it is never an excess of accumulation, but a precise orchestration created for human perception.

What we seek to transcend is the impoverished imagination that treats people as data and homes as machines.

The twentieth-century city embraced a single metaphor: the house as “a machine for living in.”

And so we inherited spaces that were efficient yet cold, where human beings became merely another variable within a system.

What we seek to create is not a smarter machine, but a dwelling capable of perception.

Yet for centuries, our homes have remained largely unchanged — designed around static functions rather than the changing rhythms of human life.
